SECTION 01

Content Creation

HOOK GENERATOR — Basic

Give me 10 scroll-stopping hooks for a [PLATFORM] post about [TOPIC]. Target audience: [AUDIENCE] Tone: [casual/professional/controversial] Make them punchy, under 15 words each.

HOOK GENERATOR — Pattern Interrupt

Write 5 hooks that start with a controversial statement about [TOPIC]. The statement should make [AUDIENCE] stop scrolling and read. Don't be offensive — just unexpected.

HOOK GENERATOR — Story Loop

Write 3 hooks that open a story loop about [TOPIC]. The hook should create curiosity but not reveal the ending. Target: [AUDIENCE] on [PLATFORM].

CAPTION — Educational Post

Write an educational caption about [TOPIC] for [PLATFORM]. Structure: - Hook (1 line) - Problem statement (2 lines) - Solution (3-5 bullet points) - Call to action Tone: [TONE] Length: [WORD COUNT] words max.

CAPTION — Story-Based

Write a story-based caption about [TOPIC]. Include: - A personal moment or realization - The lesson learned - How the reader can apply it Tone: Authentic, conversational. No corporate speak. Target: [AUDIENCE].

CAPTION — Controversial Take

Write a hot take about [TOPIC] in [INDUSTRY]. The take should: - Challenge a common belief - Be defensible with logic - Spark discussion (not just rage) Format: Hook + 3 supporting points + open question.

THREAD OUTLINE — Educational

Create a [PLATFORM] thread outline about [TOPIC]. Structure: - Tweet 1: Hook + promise - Tweets 2-7: One insight each (bite-sized) - Final tweet: Summary + CTA Make each tweet standalone valuable. Target: [AUDIENCE].

CAROUSEL OUTLINE — Instagram

Outline a 10-slide Instagram carousel about [TOPIC]. - Slide 1: Title + hook - Slides 2-8: One point per slide - Slide 9: Summary - Slide 10: CTA + handle Design notes: Keep text minimal. Each slide should be scannable in 3 seconds.

VIDEO SCRIPT — 60-Second Short

Write a 60-second video script for [PLATFORM] about [TOPIC]. Structure: - 0-3s: Hook (pattern interrupt) - 3-15s: Problem / relatable moment - 15-45s: Solution / value - 45-60s: CTA Include visual cues in [brackets]. Tone: [TONE]

VIDEO SCRIPT — Long-Form YouTube

Outline a 10-minute YouTube video about [TOPIC]. Include: - Hook (first 30 seconds) - Intro (what they'll learn) - 5-7 main sections with timestamps - Conclusion + CTA Target: [AUDIENCE] Searchable title suggestions: 3 options.

SECTION 02

Research & Strategy

COMPETITOR ANALYSIS — Content Audit

Analyze the content strategy of [COMPETITOR/S]. Look at their last 20 posts on [PLATFORM]. Identify: - Top 3 performing content types - Their posting frequency - Common themes/topics - Gaps they're NOT covering Output: Actionable insights for my content strategy.

COMPETITOR ANALYSIS — Positioning

Compare [MY BRAND] to [COMPETITOR 1] and [COMPETITOR 2]. Analyze: - Unique selling propositions - Pricing / offer differences - Brand voice / personality - What they do better / worse than us Recommend: 3 ways I can differentiate.

NICHE VALIDATION — Market Research

I'm considering creating a product/service about [TOPIC]. Help me validate: 1. What are 5 related search terms I should check? 2. Who are 3 successful competitors? 3. What gaps exist in the current market? 4. What would make this unique? Be honest — if this is a bad idea, tell me why.

NICHE VALIDATION — Audience Profile

Create a profile of my ideal customer for [PRODUCT/SERVICE]. Include: - Demographics (age, location, income) - Psychographics (values, fears, desires) - Where they spend time online - What they've already tried - Why those solutions didn't work - What they're willing to pay for

TREND SPOTTING — Emerging Opportunities

Based on current trends in [INDUSTRY], identify: 1. 3 emerging topics gaining traction 2. 2 declining topics to avoid 3. 1 "underrated" opportunity most people miss For each, explain WHY it's trending/declining.

TREND SPOTTING — Content Calendar

Generate 30 content ideas for [PLATFORM] about [TOPIC]. Organize by: - 10 educational posts - 10 story/personal posts - 10 engagement/interactive posts Each idea should be one sentence max.

SECTION 03

Business Operations

EMAIL — Cold Outreach

Write a cold email to [TARGET] about [OFFER]. Requirements: - Subject line under 8 words - Opening that proves I did research - Clear value proposition - Soft ask (not pushy) - Under 150 words total Tone: Professional but human.

EMAIL — Follow-Up Sequence

Create a 3-email follow-up sequence for [SCENARIO]. Email 1: Sent [X days ago], no response Email 2: Send 3 days after Email 1 Email 3: Send 5 days after Email 2 Each email should add value, not just nag.

EMAIL — Sales

Write a sales email for [PRODUCT]. Include: - Story-based opening - Problem agitation - Solution introduction - 3 key benefits - Social proof placeholder - Urgency / scarcity element - Clear CTA Length: 300-400 words.

PRODUCT DESCRIPTION — E-commerce

Write a product description for [PRODUCT]. Include: - Attention-grabbing opening - 3-5 bullet point benefits (not features) - Who this is for / not for - What's included - FAQ-style objection handling Tone: [TONE] Platform: [PLATFORM]

PRODUCT DESCRIPTION — Digital Product

Write a sales page section for [DIGITAL PRODUCT]. Structure: - "What you'll learn" section - "Who this is for" section - "What's included" section - Guarantee / risk reversal Make it scannable. Use short paragraphs.

CUSTOMER RESPONSE — Complaint

Write a professional response to this customer complaint: [PASTE COMPLAINT] Requirements: - Acknowledge their frustration - Don't make excuses - Offer a specific solution - Leave door open for further conversation Tone: Empathetic but firm.

CUSTOMER RESPONSE — Positive Review

Write 5 variations of a reply to a positive review. Each should: - Thank them specifically - Reference something they mentioned - Encourage future engagement - Sound human, not templated Keep each under 30 words.

MEETING PREP — Agenda Builder

Create a meeting agenda for [MEETING TYPE]. Include: - 3-5 discussion topics - Time allocation for each - Desired outcomes - Pre-work required from attendees Meeting length: [DURATION] Attendees: [WHO]

MEETING PREP — Post-Meeting Summary

Summarize this meeting transcript into: 1. Key decisions made 2. Action items (with owners + deadlines) 3. Open questions 4. Next meeting topics [PASTE TRANSCRIPT OR NOTES]

SECTION 04

Design & Creative

THUMBNAIL — YouTube Concept

Generate 5 YouTube thumbnail concepts for a video about [TOPIC]. For each, describe: - Main text (under 5 words) - Visual element / imagery - Color scheme - Emotional tone - What makes it clickable

THUMBNAIL — Short-Form

Create 3 thumbnail concepts for a [PLATFORM] short about [TOPIC]. Requirements: - Readable in 1 second - High contrast - Curiosity-inducing - Consistent with [BRAND STYLE]

BRAND VOICE — Definition

Define a brand voice for [BRAND NAME]. Include: - 3 personality traits - What we sound like (examples) - What we DON'T sound like (anti-examples) - 5 sample phrases in our voice - Vocabulary to use / avoid Target audience: [AUDIENCE] Industry: [INDUSTRY]

BRAND VOICE — Tone Shift

Rewrite this copy in a [NEW TONE] voice while keeping the same message: [PASTE COPY] The new tone should be [ADJECTIVES].

HEADLINES — A/B Test

Generate 10 headline variations for [CONTENT TYPE] about [TOPIC]. Mix: - 3 curiosity-based - 3 benefit-driven - 3 controversial/contrarian - 1 wildcard Each under 10 words.

HEADLINES — SEO-Optimized

Create 5 SEO-optimized titles for content about [TOPIC]. Target keyword: [KEYWORD] Requirements: - Include keyword naturally - Under 60 characters - Compelling enough to click - Clear what the content delivers

A/B TEST — Email Subject Lines

Create 5 pairs of email subject lines to A/B test for [CAMPAIGN]. Each pair should test one variable: - Length (short vs long) - Tone (curious vs direct) - Personalization (with vs without) - Urgency (soft vs hard) - Benefit (feature vs outcome)

A/B TEST — CTA Buttons

Generate 10 variations of CTA button copy for [OFFER]. Mix: - Action-oriented ("Get Started") - Outcome-oriented ("Save Time Now") - Low-commitment ("Learn More") - Urgency ("Limited Spots") - Personality ("Let's Go")

SECTION 05

Personal Productivity

DAILY PLANNING — Morning Setup

Help me plan my day for maximum productivity. My priorities today: 1. [PRIORITY 1] 2. [PRIORITY 2] 3. [PRIORITY 3] Available hours: [X] Energy pattern: [MORNING/AFTERNOON/EVENING person] Create a time-blocked schedule with breaks built in.

DAILY PLANNING — Weekly Review

Guide me through a weekly review. Ask me questions about: - What went well - What didn't work - Lessons learned - Next week's priorities After I answer, summarize and give me 3 focus areas for next week.

DECISION FRAMEWORK — Pros/Cons

I'm deciding whether to [DECISION]. Help me think through: 1. Pros (what I gain) 2. Cons (what I lose/risk) 3. Hidden costs people forget 4. What would have to be true for this to be the right choice 5. What would make me regret this in 1 year Be objective. Challenge my assumptions.

DECISION FRAMEWORK — Opportunity Cost

I have [OPPORTUNITY A] and [OPPORTUNITY B] on the table. I can only choose one. Help me evaluate: - Long-term impact of each - Which aligns with my goals: [GOALS] - What I'm giving up with each choice - The "regret minimization" perspective Give me a recommendation with reasoning.

LEARNING — Topic Breakdown

I want to learn [TOPIC] as efficiently as possible. Create a learning roadmap: 1. Foundational concepts (what to learn first) 2. Intermediate skills 3. Advanced applications 4. Best resources for each stage 5. Projects to practice Timeline: [X weeks/months]

LEARNING — Concept Simplification

Explain [COMPLEX TOPIC] to me like I'm [LEVEL]. Use: - Simple analogies - Real-world examples - No jargon unless necessary - A structure: what it is → why it matters → how to use it

PROBLEM-SOLVING — Root Cause

I'm facing this problem: [PROBLEM]. Help me find the root cause using "5 Whys" analysis. For each answer, ask "why" again until we reach the core issue. Then suggest 3 solutions targeting the root cause.

PROBLEM-SOLVING — Creative Solutions

I'm stuck on: [PROBLEM]. Give me 10 unconventional solutions. Rules: - No obvious answers - At least 3 "crazy" ideas - Consider what [SUCCESSFUL PERSON/COMPANY] would do - Think about second-order effects
